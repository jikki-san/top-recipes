Salsa Martajada

About this recipe

You'd be surprised how easy making decent salsa is; it really is just as simple as charring some veggies and mashing them up. I hope this recipe gives you the confidence to ditch the Pace!

One tip to keep in mind: add one pepper at a time, tasting as you go. You can only ever increase the spice level; once you've gone too far, there's no going back. If you're into that, don't let me stop you. But if you aren't, proceed with caution.

Ingredients

  • 4 roma/plum tomatoes, halved lengthwise
  • 1 red onion, cut into quarters
  • 3 jalapeños, halved lengthwise
  • 3 whole garlic cloves
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 tbsp lime juice or red wine vinegar

Steps

  1. In a large cast iron skillet over medium heat, char the tomatoes, onion, jalapeños, and garlic, turning them over so all sides develop a light char. The onions will probably start to fall apart and stress you out; it's all good. Remember you're gonna mash all these guys in the end.
  2. Transfer the charred ingredients to a molcajete, or alternatively load them into a food processor or blender. Crush the ingredients until they're just blended; don't turn it into a soup if you can help it.
  3. Transfer the mashed, processed salsa to a serving bowl and salt to taste, then add the acid (lime juice or red wine vinegar) to balance.
  4. Dip a chip (or a restaurant-sized bag's worth, manage your own macros) and enjoy!
