Queso

About this recipe

Queso can really be a blank canvas for you to do whatever you want with. The typical base is Velveeta and Rotel, but in some ways even that is negotiable.

I've included two of my favorite add-ins here, but feel free to experiment with other items (green onions, other peppers, other veggies, etc.). The queso is your oyster.

Maybe not oyster. Don't put oyster in your queso.

Ingredients

  • 32oz Velveeta
  • 2 15oz cans Rotel, undrained
  • 1lb chorizo
  • 1 poblano pepper, stemmed, seeded, and diced

Steps

  1. Set your slow cooker to low and add the Velveeta cubes, Rotel, and 1 cup of water.
  2. Heat a nonstick skillet on medium-high, then add the chorizo. Use a potato masher to process the chorizo in the pan until all large chunks are removed (unless you're into that? Up to you, but it doesn't cook evenly). Stir occasionally to ensure it browns evenly, 5-10 minutes. Scoop the chorizo out, leaving as much oil behind as you can, and set it aside.

  4. Add the chorizo and poblano to the slow cooker and stir everything together. Give it like an hour, testing occasionally to ensure that the cheese is evenly melted and warmed through.
