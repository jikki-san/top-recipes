Guacamole

About this recipe

There's some manual labor involved here, but I promise it's worth it; nobody wants a random piece of onion showing up in their bite of guacamole. Make sure you add the tomatoes in last to ensure things don't get too watery. In addition, you should make sure not to overprocess the avocados when adding them; that'll keep your guacamole chunky.

If you have a traditional molcajete and tejolote, that's awesome; I envy you. It's great for making guac. If you don't have one, a standard mortar and pestle will do nicely. You can use a food processor or a blender if you want, but in my experience that leaves you with microscopic, bruised pieces of cilantro. YMMV ¯\_(ツ)_/¯

When you're searching for good avocados, avoid the temptation to squeeze them! This browns the inner surface of the flesh and you'll end up picking it out later. Instead, when testing for ripeness, take the small stem off the top of the avocado. If the flesh underneath is a nice greenish-yellow, you've got a winner.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 white onion, diced
  • 1 serrano pepper, diced (optionally seeded)
  • 1/2 bunch cilantro, chopped fine
  • Kosher salt (to taste)
  • 4 large avocados
  • 3 roma/plum tomatoes, cored and diced

Steps

  1. Add onion, serrano, cilantro, and a generous pinch of salt to a molcajete or mortar and pestle. Grind up the mixture with the pestle until you're looking at a questionable green paste.
  2. Take each avocado and use a chef's knife to slice it in half lengthwise, separating the two halves. Firmly (and carefully) chop down into the pit, turn your knife to release it, and pinch the pit on your blade with your knife hand to remove it. Next, taking a paring knife, score the avocado flesh 3-4 times vertically and horizontally, going all the way down to the skin, then use a spoon to scoop the resulting chunks into a large bowl.
  3. Add your questionable green onion-serrano-cilantro-salt paste into the bown with the avocado and fold it in using a wooden spoon, crushing the avocado pieces as you go.
  4. Finally, fold in the diced tomatoes until just mixed and salt to taste. Test with a tortilla chip to make sure you balance with the chip's saltiness as well.
